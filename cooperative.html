Cooperative & Thrift

The Department of Cooperatives under the Ministry of Trade, Industry, and Investment (MTII) is dedicated to fostering the effective growth of sustainable, member-initiated, and member-controlled cooperative societies. These societies play a crucial role in driving the socio-economic development of the state by creating jobs, generating income, and promoting economic diversification.

Role of the Department of Cooperative & Thrift

As a specialized and professional arm of the MTII, the department is tasked with registering and regulating the activities of cooperative societies, ensuring their alignment with established legal and regulatory frameworks.

Mission Statement

The Cooperatives Department remains committed to the consolidation, promotion, and sustainable development of cooperative societies, ensuring they contribute significantly to the socio-economic progress of the state.

Core Responsibilities

  • Promote Compliance: Ensure that cooperative societies operate within the bounds of the cooperative legal regulatory framework.
  • Support Development: Facilitate the growth and advancement of the cooperative movement by creating a favorable and enabling environment.
  • Mobilize and Educate: Lead efforts to mobilize cooperative members and provide education and enlightenment programs.
  • Regulate and Oversee: Handle registration, supervision, and inquiry into cooperative societies' activities.
  • Audit and Arbitrate: Conduct financial audits, resolve disputes through arbitration, and ensure accountability.
  • Manpower Development: Build capacity through training and manpower development initiatives.
  • Liquidation Support: Manage liquidation processes when necessary.
  • Legal and Institutional Framework: Provision of legal and institutional framework for co-operatives.
  • Co-operative Registration: Registration of co-operative societies.
  • Monitoring and Oversight: Auditing, supervision, inspection, and monitoring of co-operative societies.
  • Institutional Coordination: Coordinating co-operative activities with other institutions.
  • Policy Development: Preparation of programs and policies for the co-operative sector.
  • National Project Management: Conception, implementation, and monitoring of national co-operative development projects.
  • Foreign Assistance Coordination: Coordination of foreign financial and technical assistance for the implementation of co-operative projects.
  • Advisory Services: Advising and assisting co-operative societies and federations in the implementation of their plans and projects.
  • Data and Reporting: Data collection and publication of reports on co-operatives and the Co-operative Movement.
  • Modernization Support: Assisting co-operative societies in modernizing their activities and attaining financial and functional autonomy.
  • Human Resource Development: Promoting new management concepts through HRD, training, and education within the co-operative sector.
  • Community Advancement: Promoting and developing the Co-operative Movement in every field of human activities for the economic, social, and cultural upliftment of members and the community.
